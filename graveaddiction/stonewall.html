Stonewall Cemetery is located on Stonewall Cemetery Road, about ½ mile from US-22, outside Lancaster, Ohio (Hocking Township, Fairfield County). The coordinates are 39° 41' 28.55" N, 82° 38' 22.9" W.

Stonewall Cemetery a small family cemetery that is no longer active. It is maintained by the Fairfield County Historical Parks. The cemetery is kept locked to prevent vandalism.

The inscription above the gate gives some history about the cemetery. It reads:

THIS WALL
which encloses the family burying ground of
of NATHANIAL WILSON (one of the early Pioneers of
the West who emigrated from Cumberland County Pa and settled
near this place A.D. 1798 when all around was one continued & uninhabited
wilderness) was commenced by him A.D. 1838 & finished in the following year
by his son GUSTIN the former having suddenly died May 12 1839

The technique used to construct Stonewall Cemetery is amazing. The angles in the wall were cut into each individual stone. This is a one of a kind cemetery, there is not another one like it anywhere.

The Fairfield County parks ordered the Lebanon cedar that is planted inside. These trees are very difficult to find, this one replaced the original one.

I was told that the entrance faces the Polaris. The North star is exactly over the arch.

The cemetery is also known to be haunted. Legend has it that if you walk around the top of the cemetery's wall thirteen times at night flames will shoot up and you'll be pulled into hell!

I received the following e-mail from Lynn Scheu in August 2008, with more historical information about Stonewall Cemetery:

I just read in the 1901 C.M.L. Wiseman book, "Pioneer Period and Pioneer People of Fairfield County OH" about the "architect and builder of the famous stone wall around the graves of Nathaniel Wilson and family." He was C.F. Griner.

C.F. Griner (Charles) had some other distinctions. He married a woman who came over the mountains in a wagon with her family in 1822. She was the daughter of William Graham, a Madison and Hocking township pioneer who brought his family from PA to Fairfield County. (Wiseman didn't see fit to give her first name, as old historians were fond of not doing!)

Wiseman also saw fit to mention that he was "a special friend of the late Dr. John Williams" (the famous scholar and teacher of Greenfield Academy) and that Griner himself "was a fine scholar."

Griner "broke down soon after this work [on Stonewall} was completed and for several years he was an invalid. He died leaving a wife (the Graham baby girl who came over the mountains in 1822) and five children."

To see if you had more about the building of Stonewall Cemetery, I sought information on the web and found your site. I thought you might be interested in the builder too, so I am sending you this information, with all its sidelights. Paul Molyneux, on his website, has Nathaniel Wilson designing and building the wall himself, along with his son Gustin Wilson, who he says completed the task. Apparently he didn't know about great great great Uncle C.F. Griner.

I too was a night visitor to Stonewall back in about 1960 when I was a teen...I lived in Birmingham, Alabama then but visited family in Lancaster and hung out with friends there. Late one night we went calling on the Wilson ghosts. But in the end we were afraid to enter and so we drank 3.2 beer from the safe height of the top of the wall. Fortunately we didn't know the legend about the ghosts pulling us inside or we'd have been terrified. Nothing happened.
