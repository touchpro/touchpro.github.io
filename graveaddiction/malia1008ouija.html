Paper Board

October 11, 2008



On October 11, 2008 I received an e-mail from Malia A. about an interesting ouija board experience:


The other day at school, my friend Steve asked how to make a Ouija board. My friend Becca and I told him, then I ripped a piece of notebook paper out of my binder to sketch out what it should look like. He asked if we could try using the board I just made on the paper, and we agreed. We used a quarter instead of a normal planchette.

Now, Becca and I have used the Ouija board a few times before, so we started talking to our spirit guides and a poltergeist who hangs around us. Steve was a bit skeptical, so he started asking questions that Becca and I didn't know the answers to. The board got them all right. He then asked if he could talk to his spirit guide. His spirit guide talked to us, and told us that he was gay. I don't know if it's possible for a spirit guide to be gay, but that's what he said. By the time the bell rang to go to our sixth period class, the three of us attracted many strange looks from a teacher and a few other students.

We had so much fun talking to our spirit guides. Steve is still skeptical about the board, but we promised him that we weren't pushing the quarter. It's become somewhat of a tradition to pull out our homemade board and quarter every once and a while.
