Monday, April 26, 2004
posted at 8:10:22 PM

I can't believe it, I actually have some free time to blog tonight! I should be cleaning since I didn't get a chance to do it this weekend, but I blogging sounded like more fun. So while I'm waiting for my laundry to finish drying I'll write.

I didn't get a chance to write about it last week, but on Wednesday night I had a terrible, horrifying experience. I just got home from work, and I was reaching into the mailbox to get my mail. Well, before I could get the mail out, one of those awful big, black, hairy, jumpy spiders crawled out of the mailbox, right beside my hand!!!! I screamed and jumped back, I'm sure my neighbors in the apartment complex got a kick out of watching me! Anyway, my sudden movement frightened the spider and it crawled back inside the mailbox. That really upset me, because I didn't know how to get my mail out...there was NO way I was putting my hand in there with the spider. I came up with an idea pretty fast. I rushed inside and grabbed a fork, which I then used to pry my mail out of the box and onto the ground. I glanced inside the mailbox and the spider was still in there. I went back inside and got the Spider Killer Plus spray out of the closet. I know I've mentioned it before, but Spider Killer Plus is one of the best things ever made for people like me who can't get close enough to a spider to squash it. Anyway, I sprayed the inside of the mailbox down. I used so much spray that there was a layer of white liquid covering the entire bottom of the box :) I watched the spider writhing in pain, and finally it stopped moving and curled up into a little ball. I never did take the dead spider out of the mailbox...

So tonight I got home and brought in my mail. When I laid it on the table I noticed something small and black fall off one of the envelopes. Yeah, you guessed it...the dead spider. The thing is, I sort of forgot about it. So when I saw that small black thing move I screamed because I thought it was a living spider. I felt pretty stupid when I realized that it was the dead one. I'm just thankful no one was here to see me jump and scream :)

Hmmmm, what else has been going on? Pretty much the same old stuff. Work is still going well, and Mark still won't give in and let Pat paint the Brown Beast lime green. He obviously doesn't care about public opinion, because the majority of you voted for the LGB!

This weekend there was a Civil War reenactment at the park next to my parents house. Well, I wouldn't really call it a true reenactment since they didn't fight any battles, it was more like an encampment. They did have three cannons though, and they fired them throughout the day and once again around 9PM. My mom invited a few people down for a bonfire/cookout. It was a lot of fun! She's all ready to plan another cookout sometime in May, I'll have to invite my team from work next time.

I really can't think of anything else exciting to write about. May is shaping up to be a busy month, so hopefully I'll have more exciting things to write about then.

I think my laundry should almost be finished, so I guess it's time for me to get offline for the night. I'll try to be better about blogging this week and write again tomorrow!

Monday, April 05, 2004
posted at 7:49:32 PM

My first day back to work wasn't too bad...I figured it would be terrible to go back after being out for seven days (not including the weekends!). I'm so glad I was wrong though. My area moved up a floor last week (I moved my stuff before I left), so today was also my first day at the new desk...I really like it. The desk is so neat and clean right now, but it'll probably only be a couple days before it's a mess again :)

The last few days of my vacation at home weren't so great. I'm so congested, it's either allergies, a sinus infection, or a cold. Benadryl seems to help though, so I'm not going to waste my time going to the doctor as long as it clears up within a couple weeks. Like I told everyone at work today, it's probably just a reaction from coming home to 40-50 degree rainy weather (it was in the 80's and sunny in New Orleans!). I was having an awful time with losing my voice this morning though. I sounded pretty bad, but fortunately it got a little better as the day went on.

I just remembered that I forgot to blog about my exciting adventure on Saturday. Thursday (night) and Friday were the worst days for my cold/sinus/allergies. I was still feeling pretty bad when I woke up on Saturday morning after the maybe two hours of sleep I had, but I told Ryan that Chuck (for those of you who have never heard me refer to the vehicle by name, Chuck is my Chevy truck!) and I would go over to his place to help him haul some old garage doors and miscellaneous junk to the dump. John came to help us too. After eating a great breakfast at Canal Winchester's Wigwam restaurant (it's one of those fun hole-in-the-wall type places in downtown Canal), we were back to Ryan's place to load all the junk into the bed of the truck. There was a ton of stuff to haul away, the bed was filled. We tied everything down, then it was off the the dump near Grove City. Now, I had never been to a dump, so I wasn't sure what to expect. But it was WAY worse than I would've ever imagined! And John and Ryan felt the same so I didn't feel too ignorant :) We drove up to the booth to pay, but the window in the trailer was really high so I had to jump out of the truck to pay. That made all of us laugh...but then as we started to drive things got funnier! John and I assumed there would at least be concrete roads to drive on...but instead it was just a wide, muddy dirt path with big ruts/potholes everywhere, with mountains of trash looming in front of us! I tried to follow the much bigger Ford truck a little ways in front of me to avoid some of the potholes, but there was really no way to avoid them. I kept worrying that poor Chuck would get stuck in the mud, but we finally made it to drier ground. Well, actually "ground" isn't a good word to use...we made it to drier trash. I was driving on piles of mud and white colored trash - we're guessing it was trash treated with some sort of chemical to help it disintegrate faster? Honestly, we couldn't tell what it was! Anyway, I was stopped on the trash pile waiting for the guy in front of me to back up. I was watching his tires as he was backing up, and I could see how squishy/spongey the ground under him was by the way the tires were sort of bouncing. He finally finished backing up, then it was our turn. John and Ryan helped me decide where to stop, they were worried about the truck getting stuck too. Well, we stopped way in front of the other people, we figured we could just carry the trash back to the piles ourselves. But before we could even start lifting the trash, the guy driving this big piece of machinery with spikey wheels started honking at us and motioning for us to back up further. Since he was so persistent in honking, I had to get in, start the truck up, and back it up more. The guy was probably afraid that we were going to dump it right where we were...but we were really just scared of getting stuck! Since the three of us didn't feel too comfortable there, we unloaded the bed of the truck at light speed, then we got the hell out of there! We laughed about our adventure the whole way back to Ryan's house. Ryan paid for my breakfast, but he said that he owes me a car wash and new shocks after that fun-filled journey :) Honestly though, I really do need to wash the truck, it's filthy...there's mud caked around the tires. I can say this much...the dump definitely isn't a place that I'd like to visit again if I can help it! :) I'm always willing to help out my friends though!

So now that I've bored you with this weekend's great adventure, I think I'm going to call it a night!
