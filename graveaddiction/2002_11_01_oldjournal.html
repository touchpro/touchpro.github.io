This past Friday my sister bought me a gift from World Market:



Yes, my sister bought me slime...but not just any slime, she bought me Eyeballs 'n Goo. Eyeballs 'n Goo is special to me, because it reminds me of my senior year high school English class. That may seem strange, but here's the story. Chrissie, the friend I mentioned about a week and a half ago when I was talking about the opera, was in the same English class. I believe it was our third period class, but that's really not important. Chrissie carried her backpack with her to all of her classes. She had her books and other class supplies in that backpack, but she also carried lots of fun things: silly putty, play doh, whiteout (that's another story!) and of course eyeballs 'n goo. Everyday in English she would select one of these fun things and annoy us entertain us by playing with them during class. One day she decided to play with her eyeballs 'n goo. She cleared off a space on her desk, which was to my left, and let the goo run all over her desk. Part of it even started to drip off the edge. Well, to see the chalkboard I had to look to my left, and most of the time I kind of sat sideways in my desk. And since I have big feet, my feet were located right under the edge of her desk. I was wearing my favorite red shoes that day, so you can only imagine how angry I was when I noticed that I had green goo that had dripped off her desk on my shoes!! Chrissie laughed and laughed and laughed about it. Which didn't make me any less angry! Anyway, I always think of Chrissie and my red shoes when I see eyeballs 'n goo. Maybe I need to go to World Market and buy a jar for her...but I have new goo-free red shoes now, maybe I shouldn't get her a jar of eyeballs 'n goo!! I want to keep these shoes safe!

Tonight I started my search for a new artificial Christmas tree. I didn't find anything I liked at Garden Ridge, but I didn't go anyplace else to continue my search. I don't know what's wrong with me, I just don't feel festive! The thought of continuing my quest for the tree just depresses me, so I think I may wait until after Christmas to find a new tree. I'm going to put up the old one that fell over last year, but instead of pushing all of the ugly branches to the front to make it look fuller like I did last year I'm going to try something else...tinsel. I'm not a big fan of tinsel, but I think it may help close the gaps between the branches without weighting down one side of the tree. I think I'll try doing that this weekend. My worst fear is that I'll get it all decorated and it will fall over just like it did last year. But I'll try to keep positive.

I just looked outside...it's been snowing all evening, and it's cold enought that it's sticking to the grass and trees. It looks so pretty! Hopefully it's not sticking to the roads!

As if my normal battles with insomnia weren't bad enough, my efforts to sleep will be much more complicated tonight due to the spirit's presence. Not too much has happened since I've been home from work, but here's the most recent events:


  1. The first thing I did when I got home tonight was check out the bookcase that I draped one of the lace tablecloth things on. I have 2 copper taper candle holders on the front corners of the bookcase. They have been in the same place for the last two years, they never move from where I put them. Tonight the left candle holder was moved, and about a quarter of an inch of it was hanging off the edge. I moved it back, and a few minutes later it was back near the edge again. So far it's stayed where I put it.
  2. Next thing that happened involved a window. First, you need to know that I rarely turn my thermostat up or down...I set it and leave it. I called my mom and told her about the candle holder. After I finished talking to her I ran upstairs to change out of my work clothes. It only took me probably 2-3 minutes to change. I went back downstairs to start cooking dinner. When I got downstairs I noticed that my window had fogged up. It was all foggy except for one strip right down the center, vertical from the top to the bottom. I thought it might be due to a temperature change, but since I didn't adjust the thermostat and since the sun wasn't hitting that window I determined that wasn't it. Also, that strip down the middle didn't make sense either.
  3. Last but not least...this is the second time I've tried posting about this. The first time my computer just turned off while typing the third sentence. My computer has turned off on it's own before, but usually when that happens on the laptop the power indicator for the battery is shut off. Not so tonight, the battery light didn't even flicker.

I've been trying to think about other things. I'm usually not a paranoid person, but tonight I've been just awful. I keep seeing shadows, and I'm sure it's not the spirit, just my imagination. I took two Alluna Sleep pills, they're the best non-addictive sleeping aid ever! Hopefully that'll help me get some rest!

I talked with Chrissie tonight, her ankle is a little better I guess. It still sounds terrible to me! Ankle injuries are bad, I broke my left one back in high school. It was pretty embarassing. I went to a Catholic high school. The public schools bused us there. The high school kids got out first, then we drove to the elementary/junior high school and had to wait about half an hour or 45 minutes (I can't remember, it was about 7 years ago that I rode the bus!) until they got out. Instead of sitting on the buses, we'd all walk around the first floor and outside of the school. One autumn day, I think it was a Wednesday or Thursday, a bunch of us were walking down the stairs in front of the school on our way back to the buses. I was talking and not paying much attention. I was on the last step...one more step and I'd be on the nice, flat, level sidewalk. And that's when it happened...I tripped on my own big feet and fell right in front of my friends! I was absolutely mortified, but I decided to laugh about it. Then I tried to get up. I felt a terrible pain shoot up my leg. That's when I noticed that my foot was bent in towards my body. That's just about the time panic struck. A few friends ran in the school and came back out with the school secretary. She saw my ankle and ran back in (as fast as a 70 year old could run) to get the school nurse. While I was waiting for what seemed like an eternity for the nurse, a group of second graders and their teacher ran over to see if I was OK. Being surrounded by a bunch of kids asking questions while waiting for the nurse was quite embarassing for a sophomore in high school. The nurse finally came and took me inside. She took off my shoe to look at the ankle, and then gave me an ice pack. Then it was time for me to put my shoe back on and get back on the bus. However, when she tried to get my shoe back on it wouldn't fit...my ankle was that swollen! So I had to leave it off. No big deal...until I got home and had to get off the bus with my shoe, the backpack, and my tenor sax! What a mess! And as if that wasn't bad enough, I had to get my mom to write me a note to get me out of gym class. She wrote the note and sealed it. I trusted her and thought nothing of it. I gave it to the gym teacher/football coach the next day and he cracked up laughing...turns out my mom decided to be humorous. The letter said:

"Dear Mr. XXXXX,

Please excuse Beth from gym class today. She was struck down on the steps of St. Mary's yesterday in punishment for her many sins. Her ankle is quite swollen, and the only activity she can participate in is the one legged limp relay.

Thank you"

I couldn't believe it. It seemed like everyone heard about that note, even though only about 5% of our tiny school probably did hear about it! That was just one of those traumatic moments I'll never forget!

Anyway, I'm starting to feel a little tired, and I hear my bed calling my name. I think I'll go and get some sleep (hopefully)!!

I decided to check the house out, which was a stupid thing to do if someone had broken in. I am positive that the noise did not come from next door. I hear things next door, and they are a little muffled, this was definitely a knock on my bedroom door! The first thing I did was check outside, but there were no people or animals that could have made the knocking sound. I also checked to make sure all the doors and windows were locked. Everything seemed fine. I opened all the doors, the shower curtain, cupboards...everything! There was nothing in my house.

When I got to work I called my mom to tell her my story. She said it was weird, and then she said "I bet it's your great grandmother, you took the lace home this weekend". Last Saturday (11/9) a bunch of us were looking through stuff at my great uncle's house. I found two old lace tablecloths (they're about the size of tablecloths anyway, but the ornate lacework is only on one edge so I'm not sure exactly what they were used for) and two small handmade pillows. They more than likely belonged to my great grandma. Mom washed them for me and kept them until I took them home this Saturday. Mom told me that a bunch of things happened while the lace was in her house. She kept seeing shadows, and Sammie (one of the dogs) and Splotches (the cat) acted strange. Sam just acted nervous and spooked, and the cat kept staring at unseen things. Like I said, I took the lace and pillows home with me on Saturday. Mom said she didn't see any shadows yesterday. Also, Sammie and Splotch were acting normal again (well, as normal as they can anyway!). However, now "things" are happening at my house!!! In addition to the "knocking", I also kept seeing things moving out of the corner of my eye yesterday, but when I'd turn to look nothing was there. I blamed it sun hitting the windows and throwing shadows, but since there was no sun yesterday (it was a normal overcast Ohio day!) that probably wasn't it. I am totally freaked out! I love reading about ghost stories, watching these stories on TV, or even visiting haunted places, but it's really different when you're the one actually witnessing this sort of stuff! At least this is a nice spirit. I won't have to worry about oversleeping ever again!

I wasn't overly impressed with The Magic Flute. There were some humorous scenes, but I thought the plot was pretty lame. I'm glad I saw it, but it ranks on the bottom of my favorite operas list. Cheesy Chicken Enchiladas


  • 1 can Campbell's® Cheddar Cheese Soup
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 2 cups chopped cooked chicken
  • 3/4 cup Pace® Picante Sauce
  • 1 tsp. chili powder
  • 8 flour tortillas (8 inch)


Directions:
MIX soup and milk.
MIX 2 tablespoons soup mixture, chicken, picante sauce and chili powder.
Spread 1/2 cup soup mixture in 3-quart shallow baking dish.
Spoon about 1/4 cup chicken mixture on each tortilla.
Roll tortilla around filling and place seam-side down in baking dish.
Top with remaining soup mixture. Cover.
Bake at 350F for 25 minutes or until hot.

I added some shredded cheddar cheese to the chicken mixture and sprinkled some on top. I also used Blair's Death Rain Chipotle and Habanero Dry Spices instead of the chili powder. Other than those minor changes, I followed the recipe exactly.
