German Spirit

June 23, 2005



On June 23, 2005 I received an e-mail from Allison Weyler about her very interesting ouija board experience:


"I was being babysat by my step-brother's cousin, Danielle, and we decided to play with the ouija board that we had made out of a pizza box the night earlier. We sat down at the dining room table with the ouija board in front of us, along with a bowl of water and some flowers. We had read on the internet that spirits liked flowers and water. We put our index fingers on the guitar pick that we used as our planchette.

Danielle: Are there any spirits in the room?
Board: No

Danielle: Are there any spirits here?
Board: Yes

Danielle: What is your name?
Board: Kne

Allison: Is your name Ken?
Board: Yes

Allison: What is your last name?
Board: Zfxiyhxp

Danielle: Are you a good spirit or an evil spirit?
Board: Good

Allison: Who are you here to talk to?
Board: Danilieisza

There is a bang as something falls to the tile floor. Both of us run upstairs in fright. After a while, we venture downstairs. Danielle puts on a Beatles song and we consult the computer. We looked up the word 'Danilieisza' on Google search, and all the sources were in some other language. Danielle suggested that it was German, and we found an online German to English dictionary. One of the things that 'Danilieisza' meant was 'Danielle's', and came to the conclusion that Ken must be German. We didn't know if he was spelling 'Danielle' and accidentally added the 's', or if he was going to say Danielle's friend (me), or something like that. Danielle didn't want to talk to him again, so I put the Ouija board away."
