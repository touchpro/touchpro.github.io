Monday, December 23, 2002
posted at 11:13:39 PM

Today was a great day at work...I got to leave at 12:30! Leaving early always makes the day a great one!

This evening I met Chrissie and Jennifer to exchange gifts. We had a good time, and I really like the gifts they got me! After our gift exchange I drove to my parents' house since I was already half way there. My sister's boyfriend made lasagna for them (my mom swears he makes the best lasagna ever) and there was a lot leftover so I got to take some home for lunch tomorrow.

Now for sad news: This weekend when I went down to my parents' place to help bake cookies, I noticed something different when I got out of the truck. It didn't take me long to realize what was different - Cherokee, my dog that we had for about fourteen years, was nowhere to be seen. His house wasn't outside at the top of the hill anymore. I knew that one of two things happened: 1) They moved his house closer to the barn so he'd stay warmer, or 2) he died. He had hip/leg problems and when I let him loose at Halloween he had a terrible time walking. Instead of asking my parents I decided to wait to see if they said anything to me first. A few hours later they still didn't say anything, so when I was alone in the kitchen with my sister and her boyfriend I asked Katy "when did Cherokee die". She looked surprised and told me they put him to sleep on 12/13...over a week ago! Katy said mom didn't want to tell me. So I walked into the living room and calmly asked "so when were you going to tell me about Cherokee". My mom and dad both got that "deer in the headlights" look, and explained that they didn't want to tell me because they didn't want me to be upset and think they were murderers or something. Of course I wouldn't have thought that! They liked Cherokee too, and they wouldn't put him to sleep unless he was in pain. Mom also said that I told her not to tell me.....which is not technically true. I told her not to ask for my opinion as to whether or not they should put him to sleep, but I kinda assumed they would tell me afterwards! Anyway, sounds like they were both extremely upset about having to put him to sleep. Mom even cried at work when she thought about it, which is another reason why I'm surprised I didn't find out sooner. I have three friends that work with my mom, and all three knew about it. I talked with each of them several times last week and no one told me about Cherokee! I still can't believe it was more than a week later before I found out! It's not like I wouldn't notice he wasn't there :)

Anyway, I can't think of much else to write about. If I don't get a chance to write tomorrow, hope you all have a wonderful Christmas!!

Wait, first let me tell you that Saturday afternoon I was telling her about the unsuccessful Ouija board experience I had with Susan and Chrissie. She informed me that we probably weren't doing it right (or taking it seriously enough), and that she'd show us how it works. I was shocked to hear my mom say this, afterall, we're talking about the same lady who wouldn't let me bring a Ouija board into her house back when I still lived at home. Anyway, back to her dream last night. Mom said that she had a dream that a person who passed away back when she was in high school came and told her that she should not use the Ouija board. Way back in the late 1960's my mom and a friend tried to contact a classmate that had died in a train wreck. They asked to hear a train whistle to know whether or not the person they were contacting was present. However, when they looked outside there was no train in sight, and the whistle sounded like it was right by the house. Then the message indicator on their Ouija board started spinning around like crazy! It scared my mom and her friend to death, which is why my mom never wanted me to play with one. My mom said that the guy in her dream last night was this same guy they tried contacting years ago, and that he was even wearing the suit he was buried in. Pretty strange. Needless to say, she said she will NOT be showing us how to use the board correctly!

This week has been going really fast...and I can't believe it's the week before Christmas! I only have one present left to buy, which really isn't bad at all. Of course, I probably won't be saying it "isn't bad at all" after fighting with the crowds to get that last gift! posted at 12:39:37 PM

Nevermind waiting til tonight to blog, I've decided to go ahead and blog during lunch. I was supposed to have lunch with a friend here at work, but she called this morning from home and she sounds really sick. She hopes to be back to work tomorrow though. I always enjoy lunch with Cassandra...we worked together in LHS (Life and Health Systems) a few years ago, and now she works in IAS too!

My hands are extremely dry today. Let me backup and explain:

This past Thursday (12/12) I went to an auction down in Rushville at Clum Auctions, Inc. They have a big barn that they hold auctions in each month. This auction had a lot of glassware advertised, so I decided to take the day off work and go. We got there around 8:30AM and looked around all of the stuff in the inside, heated lower part of the barn. There was lots of kitchen depression glass, glass rolling pins, antique furniture, etc. Basically, all of the really, really expensive stuff was down there. While my mom and I were looking around downstairs my dad went and checked out the upper part of the barn and all of the primitives outside. He came back downstairs and told us that most of the stuff up there was junk like old, dirty glass. The "old dirty glass" statement intrigued me, so I went to check it out around 9:00. And I'm glad I did...the upper, unheated part of the barn contained tables filled with boxes of old Anchor Hocking Fire King stuff from the 1940's-1970's...which is the stuff I collect!! They auctioned off all of the primitives and stuff that were sitting outside on the grass first, then they made it to the box lots in the barn sometime between 1:30-2:00. After paying $18 for 9 big boxes of glassware around 4:30 I decided to check out. There were a few more boxes I wanted to bid on, but 9 boxes is a lot of glass and one can only cram so many boxes into a car! I found quite a few surprises in the boxes, which was quite exciting. For example, in one box that I thought just contained a bunch of Fire King brown Kimberly mugs, I found a pair of Anchor Hocking Early American Prescut Individual Shakers!!! Those alone were worth way more than $18.

Of course, you're probably still wondering why my hands are so dry and cracked. Well, like my dad said, those boxes were full of "old, dirty" glass. Some of the glasses even had dead bugs in them, which was quite disgusting. So I needed to wash everything in the nine boxes. I spent about 4 hours on Saturday night doing dishes!!! I even used Palmolive since it's not supposed to dry out hands, but that's probably only true for normal usage and 4 hours of doing dishes definitely isn't normal!! All of the glass looks nice, clean, and shiny now so it was definitely worth dry hands!

I didn't spend my whole weekend doing dishes though! Friday night I went to the Battelle Holiday Mixer with Chrissie and Susan (who both work there). Battelle's holiday mixer was very nice - they rented out the ballroom at the Buckeye Hall of Fame Cafe, provided sandwiches, appetizers, beer, and wine...all for free! They also rented buses to shuttle people from Battelle to Buckeye Hall of Fame. Pretty cool. I got to meet some of their coworkers that I hear them mention from time to time, and I even got to see their office and labs. That was really cool, because I never toured a non-school science lab before.

After the mixer we went back to my place to try out the Ouija board. We lit a single black taper candle, turned out the lights, turned off the phone, and placed two fingers each on the message indicator...and absolutely NOTHING happened! Perhaps it's because Susan and I both laughed, or perhaps because Chrissie was the only one whodidn't doubt that the board would work. Only three questions were answered:


  1. Chrissie asked how many spirits were in my house. The board said 8.
  2. Chrissie asked for a name, and the board slowly (and I do mean painfully slow!!) spelled out the name "Lex". Which might have been a spirit pretending to be my great grandfather, because all of the old letters I have addressed to him by friends refer to him as "Leck". It was still kinda creepy.
  3. Chrissie also asked if one of the people who works with her (and Susan) is gay. The board said no.

Even though this experience made me doubt the Ouija board even more, I still plan to have the Ouija party at my place early next year. Some of the friends I have invited have had good results with the board so maybe the next time we'll have better results.

Anyway, I don't want to spend my entire lunch break blogging...I want to get some work done so I don't end up spending the night here!

The psychic party turned out great! I always worry about being a bad hostess and having parties that people don't enjoy, but everyone seemed to have lots of fun!! But before I go into details about the party, let me tell you about the stuff the psychic/medium told me...


  1. Gayle told me that I do have spirits/ghosts in my apartment, but none are harmful. She says that some are family, but most are just spirits that are attracted to me. She says I have lots of positive energy around me, and spirits seem to follow me home. She said that quite a few are from my cemetery explorations! I found it very interesting that when she got to my place, the first thing she looked at was my great grandmother's lace tablecloth...she went over to it without me saying a word about it!
  2. This is related to the ghost stuff, but she also assured me that I'm not having a breakdown. She couldn't have possibly known that I was worried about that just from looking at me. She said that the shadows and little twinkly lights I see around the apartment are spirits. She said my psychic abilities are really starting to open up, and she said she's excited because they should start to open up more. Gayle thinks I am very psychic and gave me a few tips on developing my abilities. The other psychic I saw over two years ago said similiar things about me.
  3. Gayle told me that she does see me getting married someday, but she also said she thought it was strange since she also sensed this was something I didn't want to hear. Which is true, I'm not one of those normal girls that dreams about getting married. Guess I'm abnormal. She doesn't see me getting married anytime soon, but probably in my late twenties. I thought that was interesting too, because the other psychic also told me that she saw me getting married, and she guessed around the time I turn 28.
  4. She said she wasn't sure about kids. She sees me maybe having one, but not more than that. I still can't imagine myself having any. Don't get me wrong...I like kids, but only when they can talk. Babies absolutely terrify me. People always guess that it's because babies seem fragile, but that's not it. It's that whole talking thing, when they're upset I need them to tell me what's wrong, not cry! Anyway, that's another discussion, let's get back to the psychic!
  5. Another thing she told me was to stop worrying about my job. She says I don't need to worry about getting laid off. I guess I kinda knew that, but I always worry about "what if?". Tied to that, she also told me to stop worrying about my family members getting laid off, we'll all keep our jobs.
  6. Gayle also told me that I need to get a part time job doing something that interests me and would be fun. Not for the money so much, but to get me out of the house. She said that I'm one of those people that needs a purpose to get out, just going out drinking isn't purpose enough. Which is very true, but all of my friends acted surprised when I told them after my reading, so I guess this isn't common knowledge about me. Maybe I'll consider doing that, I do think working at an antique store or even a pet store could be fun!
  7. One thing she said almost scared me. Well not really, just the way she started the statement. She said "I don't know how to tell you this, I don't want to offend you...", which made me think it was going to be something bad. But it wasn't, she just told me that I seem to be more pagan than Christian. It made me laugh, because I already know that! I don't even pretend to be a Christian anymore. I don't go and tell everyone I'm pagan though, I usually just wait until it comes out in a conversation. So many people around here are born again Christians that I can't openly talk about my religious beliefs for fear that they'll burn me at the stake or something! OK, maybe I'm exagerating a little...they wouldn't think about starting the Salem witch trails again, but they would give me "you'll burn in the everlasting fires of hell" and the "I'll pray for your soul" speeches that I get so very tired of listening to!

Everyone else had good readings too. Just about everyone was impressed and wants me to have another psychic party soon. I thought it could be an annual thing, but they're pushing me to make it a semiannual thing! We'll see about that, there's a lot of prep work involved for me!

Also, I think I've talked a few friends into having an "Ouija Party" at my place, sometime after the holidays when it's boring because the weather is gray and dreary and there's nothing to do. Since I supposedly have lots of spirits living here this could be lots of fun!!

Now that the party is over, I should have time to start blogging on a regular basis again!
