Molland

March 14, 2006



On March 14, 2006 I received an e-mail from Craig Diessel about his interesting ouija board experience:


"Thought that I would share a story with you that my sister (33 years old) and myself (27 years old) were involved in. She had been hassling me for a few hours to have a seance, but I refused. After a while I gave in on one condition, that we DO NOT call up our father who had crossed over some 2 years earlier. She agreed and we proceeded.

Me: Is there anyone that would like to speak with us?
Board: Yes

Me: Can you tell me your name?
Board: MFD (that is the initials of our father)

I proceeded to close the board down in which I was very angry and upset as I thought my sister had done this. She was also crying and swore that it wasn't her.

About 20 mins later she convinced me to try again. We did and it was very odd.

Me: Is there anyone there?
Board: MOM, MOM, MOM

Me: Who are you?
Board: No response

Me: Where are you from?
Board: Molland

Me: Molland?? Where is that?
Board: Molland

Me: Is that Holland?
Board: Yes

Me: Do you have something to say?
Board: MOM, MOM, MOM

We continued to ask certain questions about age, date of death, etc but I cannot recall exact answers. I remember doing the math and it making sense. However I never understood, "MOM." I'm not sure whether MOM means mother in Holland."
