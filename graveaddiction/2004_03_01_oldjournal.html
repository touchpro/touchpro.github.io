Happy Saint Urho's Day!!!

Never heard of Saint Urho? You're not alone, I didn't know the story until I received the Old Farmer's Almanac newsletter in my inbox this afternoon! Here's the brief description from the newsletter:

* St. Urho's Day (March 16)

The legend of St. Urho began in northern Minnesota in the 1950s and grew among Finnish-Americans to the point where he is now known across the United States and Canada, and even in Finland. The legend says that St. Urho chased the grasshoppers out of ancient Finland, thus saving the grape crop and the jobs of Finnish vineyard workers. His feast is celebrated by wearing royal purple and nile green.

From visiting www.sturho.com (which I highly recommend!) I learned that he chased the grasshoppers out by saying the phrase "Hein‰sirkka, hein‰sirkka, mene t‰‰lt‰ hiiteen", which is roughly translated "Grasshopper, grasshopper, go to Hell". That sounds similar to what my sister says every summer when we walk through the fields around my parents' house...she hates grasshoppers!!
