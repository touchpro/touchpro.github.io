Where have I been? I've been SICK! I'm feeling much better today though. I started feeling a little sick on the way home from the Moonville Tunnel early (very early!) Monday morning. When I woke up at 5:30 Monday morning to get ready for work I felt even worse. So I ended up calling in sick on Monday and Tuesday.

I think I should have called in sick again today. I think the terrible storm we had was a sign for me to stay home...but I ignored it. My twenty minute drive turned into a 40 minute drive, the storm was terrible! Really hard rain, loud thunder, and lots of lightning. You know a storm is bad when people are driving 25-35mph on I-270! If I had been sitting at home I would have taken some digital photos of the lightning...it was the really streaky kind that lasted for a long time. I swear, my drive to work was like driving with strobe lights, the lightning kept flashing. I love storms, I just don't like driving in them!

My trip to the Moonville Tunnel was a lot of fun! There were just four of us, counting myself. We left Columbus around 4:30 and made it to the Lake Hope Furnace around 5:45. It was probably another 15 minutes or so before we made it to the gravel road near the tunnel. We parked the cars and then walked to the cemetery. It was a very small cemetery, but it was really cool since it was in such a desolate, isolated location. Then we walked along Racoon Creek to the tunnel. Vinton County is basically just a big forest, so the woods we walked through was very dense. When we came to a hill, Chris told me that the tunnel was up at the top, but the trees and weeds were so thick that I couldn't even see it! We stayed at the tunnel until about 10:30-11:00PM. It was pitch black back there, I could barely see the people sitting right next to me! We didn't hear or see any ghosts, but we did hear all sorts of wildlife, including an owl. We stopped at Steak 'n Shake to eat on the way back, and I finally made it home at 2:22AM. It really was fun, I'll try to post the pictures on the site tonight!

    I'm going to Pennsylvania in September. I'm also going back to West Virginia in September for the 2nd Annual Mothman Festival.
