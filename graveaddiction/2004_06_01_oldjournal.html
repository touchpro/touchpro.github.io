Well OK, I manage to talk about cemeteries everyday :) I am really tired tonight though. Not so much because of work, but because I stayed up later than I planned to last night. I went over to Mark's place so he could help me check out a fuse in the truck, and so he would be there if I ended up needing help changing the truck's running light bulb. Not only did we work on Chuck (the Truck), but we went to a cemetery, ate dinner, and watched an episode of the Dukes of Hazzard. It was a fun night.

I stopped at Meijer's since it's on the way home from Mark's place. I bought bunches of fruit to make fruit salad, and I had to pick up smarties (you know, the roll candies) since Mark's birthday was one of the ones being celebrated and that's one of his favorite candies. It was worth it though, I thought the fruit salad turned out awesome!

Tonight I got the truck's A/C recharged. It hadn't been working for awhile - it would blow out air, but not cold air :( That's why Mark and I checked out the fuse last night. Believe it or not, waiting for the A/C to be recharged wasn't too bad. The mechanic let me watch, and he did a good job explaining everything that was going on. Car stuff really intrigues me, so I enjoyed that. My A/C is working fine now. They didn't find any leaks, so hopefully it will continue to work great for a long time! And to make the "adventure" even better...I noticed they had trouble finding pens when I went to sign the receipt. So you know what I did? I gave them a couple Grave Addiction pens to use at the shop!!! Yeah, I'm a geek when it comes to the site! Oh OK, fine...I'm a just a geek in general :)

When I got home I called this lady in Vinton County who needed to interview me for a school project. She's taking a powerpoint class, and her professor wanted the class to do their presentations on haunted places. How cool is that?! I would have loved to have an assignment like that! Anyway, she had a bunch of good questions for me, and she's also planning to share some of her research about Elisabeth's Grave with me to use on the site!

So overall it's been a good couple of days. Now I just can't wait until this weekend...cemetery exploring in Gallipolis, OH and Mason County, WV!!!!! But I thought I'd make time to write tonight. No particular reason, I just felt like I needed to get a post out here for June :) I'm not going to write about everything that's been going on the past couple weeks, I think I'll just start with today.

The highlight of the day was lunch with my friend Thiru, he's one of the programmers I used to work with in my old area. It was great to talk with him and catch up, and lunch was awesome...we went to the Hunan Lion at Mill Run. I had heard wonderful things about it, but today was the first time I went there. I had Hunan Chicken. It was really good, and quite spicy. I love hot food, I have a pretty high tolerance to spicy stuff. But my heat tolerance was nothing compared to Thiru...he got his Hunan Chicken extra spicy AND added hot sauce to it. I was impressed, there's no way I could have eaten that stuff extra spicy and enjoyed it!

I also talked to some of my other friends from the old area today. I went upstairs to drop some of the Grave Addiction pens off at Thiru's desk. While I was up there I decided to stop by my friend Karen's desk to talk about some photos I found on the internet. The photos were of someone we both know, and that's all I'm going to say. While I was talking with her a bunch of my other coworkers stopped by. It's always fun to talk with them. Anyway, that's about all that happened today. Well, I did talk to my mom on the phone tonight about her grandchildren. Well, I should say her LACK of grandchildren.
