Flemming Petersen sent me the following information about this abandoned house:

An old farmhouse located near village Pederstrup in the Højreby Community. 1880-1910 architecture. The house has been abandoned since about the mid 1970's based on some old Danish crown caps found in the house, and the remaining stove and toilet. When you have made your way through the shrubbery, it is a surprisingly friendly and light house you are getting into, but still it is abandoned and worth a picture. You of course get into a very little hall and you have three possibilities. If you go to the left you go into an old kitchen with two stoves: one old firewood stove, and the other a newer gas cooker. The kitchen is very small and the floor is covered with chalck and old newspapers. From the kitchen you can go right, which is the entrance to an old dirty, brown toilet, covered with chalck. If you choose to go left when standing in the hall, you get into an old living room, where I got a lot of de-ja-vus, which was quite spooky as I have never been in the house before. The walls are covered with old, brown-patterned wallpaper, and the floor is a woody floor covered with chalck from the collapsing ceiling and a lot of holes you can easily fall into and break a leg if you are not careful. When you stand in the hall, the third choice is to use the stairs and go up to the first floor. The first floor is one big empty room, except from a really big closet with a mirror in the middle. The closet was locked, but my brother got it open at last; unfortunately in only contained an iron bar, and an old hanger. There's also a barn belonging to the house, which is full of old rusty tools, and hundreds of jars with screws and other things like that. It was here that my brother also found an old milk can filled with crown caps that helped us date the last owners of the house. Before 1974, as there were crown caps from a brewery that closed exactly that year.
