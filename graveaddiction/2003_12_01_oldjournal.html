Monday, December 29, 2003
posted at 5:34:55 AM

Hope you all had a great Christmas!

I had a fun Christmas, it was a very busy weekend. I spent Christmas day with my family. We did the normal stuff - opened presents, ate roast beef for dinner, and went to the annual family get together with my mom's side of the family. My parents got me a lot cool gifts. I'd have to say my favorite though would have to be the "Jackass" calendar my mom found at Tractor Supply (they just love shopping there for stuff for their barn). It's a calendar with jackass (donkey) photos. The photos have captions like "when I grow up I want to be an asstronaut", etc. Mom thought it would be fun for me to hang up at work as a sort of statement :) I'll be sure to hang it up ASAP on January 2 when I get back to work after New Year's.

Friday I went to a few cemeteries around Lancaster and Sugar Grove in Fairfield County. I added the new photos to the site last night, you can see them by clicking here. It was a gorgeous day, it was really bright and sunny. In fact, the whole weekend was like that, and the temperatures were much warmer than they had been.

Saturday my parents came up to my place to visit. We had lunch and went to see The Return of the King. It was awesome!! I loved all three Lord of the Rings movies, I don't think I could pick a favorite.

Saturday night/Sunday morning I took down the Christmas tree and all the other decorations. It's nice to have my apartment back to normal, I'm not a big fan of Christmas decorations.

Yesterday afternoon/evening I went to a couple cemeteries in Delaware County (outside Bellepoint). Both were really beautiful. The first one, Mill Creek Cemetery, was back a quiet country road. The cemetery had a low stone wall around it, and it was surrounded by lots of trees. Mill Creek runs behind the cemetery, down a small hill. The second cemetery I went to was Oller Cemetery. It's on Riverside Drive, across the road from the Scioto River. The cemetery is on a hill beside the church. There are plenty of old trees, and the road kind of curves around the top of the cemetery. When I saw this cemetery I had a strong feeling of déjà vu. I know I had never driven by this cemetery or seen any photos of it, but it was EXACTLY like a cemetery I was wandering around in a dream I had a few weeks ago. Too weird. I think Oller Cemetery is the most beautiful cemetery I've been to so far. I'm sure the gorgeous weather had something to do with it, but standing under the trees in the shade, looking at the sun's reflection on the river was breathtaking. The photos I took don't do it justice, but I'll be adding them to the site later this week or the beginning of next week.

But now my long weekend is over, and it's time to go back to work :( Hopefully the week will go fast, at least it's only a 4 day week due to New Year's Day being a company holiday. I'm guessing I'll probably hear about the job in Life Systems next week, my fingers are still crossed...I really hope I get it!

This morning we had our quarterly department meeting. It seemed to last for an eternity, but in reality it was only two hours. I was relieved before the meeting...a few months ago our Systems Officer suggested that Shawn and I do a presentation on the new automated testing software we're using. Luckily, he already had too many agenda items or something and we weren't asked to do a presentation. I'm a pretty shy person in front of groups, so I had no worries before going to the meeting. Towards the end of the meeting Mike, the Systems Officer, did a little ceremony of sorts to congratulate people on their accomplishments - like getting certified, etc. He also presented the top four "I Got Caught" award winners. I'm sure I've mentioned "I Got Caught" awards before so I'm sorry if I'm repeating myself - those are awards our managers give out when the "catch" us doing something good. Since I just printed all of mine out to put in my portfolio, I knew I had received fourteen this year. I figured that was about average. Well, Mike announced the fourth place winner first...he had ten IGC awards. That's when I knew I was in trouble, I was going to be "singled out" in front of the group. Honestly, I hate being recognized at work like that, I'm not exactly sure why. Turns out I was in second place, but yeah, I had to go up and accept the gift certificate in front of everyone. Oh well, it was pretty painless...it's not like I had to speak in front of the group or anything! Guess I'm just one of those people who like to remain anonymous and not have attention drawn to them. But I'm not willing to do a bad job just to avoid the attention, I can stand a little attention if it gets me raises :)

OK, on to my other issue of the day. When I came back to my desk after getting ice this morning, I noticed a little card that said "I Love Thee, Lord Jesus" with a little gold manager pin on my desk. I wasn't sure who put it there, but later found out that a certain person was giving them to people he worked with as a sort of Christmas card. OK, before I even start, let it be known that I have nothing against Christian religions, afterall, I was raised Catholic. However, as I got older I began to question the religion, and today I've rejected those beliefs...I'm not sure what I believe in. All I know is that it's important to live a good life and to be decent to everyone. So when I saw this card/pin on my desk I was a little...I can't think of the word I'm looking for. Not really offended, just displeased. Is it fair for this person who knows nothing about my beliefs to give this to me? Shawn got one too, and like he said - what if he was Muslim or Jewish? That card really might be offensive to someone of another religion. I really don't care what people believe in, but I don't think it's right to give coworkers something like that unless you know they won't be offended. And I don't think I'm overreacting since several coworkers agreed with me. My area is pretty bad about stuff like that. At last year's Thanksgiving potluck a coworker suggested saying grace as a group before we ate. One of the managers said that probably wasn't a good thing to do, someone could go to Ethics if they were offended. Another coworker than piped in and said "We ARE going to say grace, I don't think anyone in here will care, and if they did care too bad!". That statement really made me laugh! Come on, what's wrong with saying grace on your own, why must the whole group participate. It's a GOOD thing I'm not easily offended, I've had numerous religious comments directed at me personally that I could have easily used to get someone in trouble if I had ever gone to ethics - like the time someone told me I really needed to get to church for listening to "evil" music...whatever!

Like Dave, I'm feeling a little bah humbug-ish this year. I'm sure I'll have a lot of fun Christmas day though, it's always fun to watch the wiener dogs play with their new toys (as you can tell, we don't have any young children in our family, so we have to give the dogs toys!). My sister always gets her cat toys, but she usually doesn't play with them - she's too busy giving the dogs evil glances and roasting by the fire.
