Monday, October 13, 2003
posted at 6:47:38 AM

Friday night I went to the Ceely Rose play at Malabar Farm State Park. Malabar Farms was Louis Bromfield's (a Pulitzer Prize winning author) house in Lucas, Ohio (it's near Mansfield). We toured the house, which was awesome, took a wagon ride through the farm to Pleasant Valley Cemetery, had dinner, and watched the Ceely Rose Play performed by the Mansfield Playhouse. I wasn't sure what to expect, but I had a fantastic time! I'll be putting the photos on the site later this week, but I'll go ahead and give you a brief summary of the Ceely Rose story:

Malabar Farm is supposedly haunted by the ghost of Ceely Rose. In 1896 she murdered her family - her dad, mom, and brother. Why did she murder them? Well, she was obsessed with the boy next door, Guy Beery. Guy was nice to her, so in her head she came to the conclusion that he loved her and wanted to get married. However, Guy didn't want to get married! So he ended up telling her that they couldn't get married because her family didn't approve of him, which wasn't true. Ceely was so mad that she poisoned her family's cottage cheese with rat poison and served it to them for breakfast. She ended up confessing to the murders and was locked away in an institution for the rest of her life. And while some may believe the farm isn't haunted, the story is very true...there's no denying that, you can find out the info from court records and newspaper articles! I'll elaborate on the story when I add it to the site, but that's the condensed version.

Saturday my mom and I toured the Georgian, an old mansion, in Lancaster, Ohio. I had been there before, but it was a LONG time ago. We had to pick up our tickets for the Heritage Association's cemetery walk there, so we decided to take the tour since we were already there. It was a lot of fun, I'll be adding those photos to the site as well, probably next week or the week after.

Well, I need to get to work now...it's so difficult to go back after being on vacation :(
