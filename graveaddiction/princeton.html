The Princeton Cemetery is located at 29 Greenview Avenue in Princeton, New Jersey (Mercer County). The coordinates are 40° 21' 14" N, 74° 39' 36" W.

The Princeton Cemetery was established in 1757, and it still active. The cemetery is owned and very well maintained by the Nassau Presbyterian Church. There are many old gravestones, and the oldest monument still standing is that of Aaron Burr, Jr. Aaron Burr, Jr. was Vice President of the United States from 1801-1805. However, most people associate his name with the famous duel he had with Alexander Hamilton.

Many other notable people are buried at the cemetery, including Grover Cleveland, the twenty-second and twenty-fourth President of the United States. For a list of other notable people and a map, please click on this link.
