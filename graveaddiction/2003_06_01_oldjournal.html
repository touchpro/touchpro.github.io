Monday, June 30, 2003
posted at 11:22:15 PM

I've decided I'm cursed when it comes to shopping. I always try to pick the shortest line, and I almost always feel this sense of accomplishment for picking the line that will go the fastest. Well, that is always a very false sense of accomplishment, because of my checkout line curse. Like tonight, for example....I went to Meijer to pick up a few things I need for the summer reading program presentation. I got to the checkout lanes and \picked the lane with the fewest number of people waiting, and also the line where the people had just a few items to check out instead of cart fulls. But wouldn't you know it, all three people in front of me had some kind of problem checking out...their credit card wouldn't scan, a disagreement about a price, and a leaking water bottle...and the poor cashier didn't speak very good English. I just stood and watched all of the people in the longer lines around me fly through the lines! Now, I didn't blame the cashier for any of this, so when she started apologizing like crazy I told her it was no problem. Well, then she started thanking me for my patience over and over, she thanked me at least ten times! It's amazing how much happier my "no problem" response made her feel, it just goes to show it's the little things that count.

Anyway, tomorrow is the big day...the ghost presentation for the summer reading program at Newark's Emerson Miller library. I am soooooooooo nervous! I don't think the presentation should scare any of the kids. Well, a couple coworkers disagree with me about that. I'm focusing the presentation of ghost hunting, so I listed a few places where you're likely to find ghosts. Like cemeteries, historical places/museums, parks, churches, houses...and I also said "even your own backyard". I just want the kids to know that you don't have to travel far to go ghost hunting, you can find them everywhere. Well, a couple coworkers thought the own backyard part is just too scary. I totally disagree. I loved that stuff when I was a kid, and I'm certain I wasn't that abnormal. Also, my biggest argument against that being too scary is this...the PARENTS sign their children up for the presentations, and if they have a kid who is scared by stuff like this they should NOT sign the child up!!! It's no fun for me to talk about wussy ghost stuff and spoil the fun for the majority of the kids who want to hear the scary stuff. If saying that you can find ghosts in your own backyard is the scariest part of the presentation I'd have to say that it's a pretty mild presentation! I'll try to get the presentation in HTML or PDF format within the next day or too and add it to the site so all of you can read it and let me know whether or not you agree with me or the select few coworkers.

I just mentioned that I didn't think I was that abnormal. Well, Saturday night one of the police officers who was working with me at Picnic with the Pops called me and the other person working the gate with me abnormal!!!! All because we enjoy exploring abandoned buildings. Sure it may not be an extremely common activity, but check out all of the sites on my links page...we're definitely not the only people who like exploring these places! And this police officer thought my cemetery addiction is abnormal too...now that's even more common than exploring abandoned buildings!!!! Not only do "urban explorers" explore cemeteries, but so do a lot of genealogists. I still laugh when I think of her calling me abnormal...whatever! I think it just makes me more interesting!

Well, I need to go and feed all of the wildlife now. I'll blog again tomorrow to tell you how the presentation went...Wish me luck!!!!

Yesterday was a busy day! I ended up working from home - we have an objective to get one regression test plan each into TestDirector by the end of the month (this coming Monday). Well, the neighbor that startled me last night was the same person! Guess we're even now :)

Other than that, I can't think of anything else. Maybe I'll blog later this evening.

I didn't get around to making an update to the site tonight (other than this blog entry, that is!)...however, I should have a BIG, GIGANTIC update either tomorrow night or Wednesday night. Or maybe I'll do several small updates - I haven't decided yet.

Anyway, the auction on Thursday wasn't too exciting. The box lots mainly consisted of junk, and there were lots of dealers there so anything worth bidding on was going really high. So my mom and I left about 30 minutes after the auction started. I had my cameras with me (I ALWAYS have them with me!!) so we went to Sacred Heart Cemetery in Bremen. My grandma's family (my mom's mom) grew up in Bremen, and this cemetery is located up at the top of a steep hill where we have our family reunions. Every year since I was a kid we walked up to the cemetery after dinner...it was like the highlight of the family reunion! Hmmm, maybe this annual trip to Sacred Heart Cemetery as a child helped develop my cemetery addiction?!?!?!?!? Anyway, Sacred Heart is on a desolate gravel road and it's an awesome cemetery. I took plenty of pictures, so instead of going into too much detail describing it you'll just have to wait until I have the photos added to the site! :)

Anyway, after we went to Sacred Heart Cemetery we drovw down State Route 664 towards Logan. On our way there we found two other old cemeteries, Mount Tabor and Pleasant Hill. After stopping to take pictures we stopped and ate at the Olde Dutch restaurant. Then we went to the Garden Accents Factory Outlet to look for some ceramic stuff for my mom's garden. They had a lot of neat things, plus I was very excited when I saw that they had a historic kiln,"Old Number 14", that you could visit. It was built in the 1920's and it really was interesting. I took pictures of that too :)

After buying some stepping stones at Garden Accents we headed home. But on the way my mom decided to stop at Leota's place - Leota weaves baskets and sells basket making supplies...as well as other odds and ends. Leota's place is at the top of a hill near Rockbridge. We don't make it down that way too often, so it was fun to stop by. Mom found a basket that she liked, and I found two glass Fenton candle holders....for only $2.00 each!!! That's a really good deal for Fenton!!!!!! So I was pretty excited about that.

Shortly after we got back to my parents' place, my dad and sister got home from work. We went to dinner, then I went back to their place and played with the wiener dogs for awhile.

I also had Friday off. I just cleaned and stuff during the day. Well, actually my morning sucked. I got a call from the complex manager saying that the check I wrote for June's rent was returned. I totally freaked out, because I knew I had enough to cover the rent! I called the bank and after several minutes I found out that they made a mistake...or rather "the person who clears the checks was having a bad day"...and the check was returned in error. Needless to say, I was pissed!!! But they got everything fixed at least. After driving to the bank, I decided I needed to go shopping to get my mind off of everything. I went and bought potting soil and some moss so I could turn an antique egg basket into an herb planter. It looks nice, I'll have to take some pictures of it.

Friday night I volunteered at the first Picnic with the Pops of the season. It was a lot of fun, but it wasn't very busy. I do ticket sales at one of the gates, so since there is money there, the symphony hires police officers to sit with us at all of the gates. I had fun talking to the two police officers that were at my gate...we talked about all sorts of things....anything from trucks to officers having to get flea baths after going into infested houses!!!

Saturday night I volunteered at the Pops again. My parents volunteer with me on Saturday nights, we had fun that night too.

I'm finally at the end of the weekend...Sunday I drove over to the Zanesville-New Concord area to visit a few cemeteries that a person e-mailed me directions to - Crooked Creek and Carr-Ebenezer Cemeteries. They were both really old and really big. And her directions were perfect, I didn't get lost at all! I also stopped at a covered bridge that was on the way to one of the cemeteries. I got the pictures back tonight, I'll hopefully get them added to the site soon too.

It was really hard to go back to work today. Four days away from the office is a long time! But I made it through the day, and I'm ready to tackle tomorrow...I forgot that I told my manager I'd do a presentation on HTT (High Throughput Testing) for our team meeting. I'm not too worried about it, I'll be able to throw something together tomorrow!

Anyway, I feel like I've been blogging for hours now, so I'm going to call it a night!

I've read on several ghost related sites that a change in state will sometimes result in strange paranormal activity. For example, if you bring an antique rocking chair home with you from a house that it had sat in for several years, that is a change of state. Well, it doesn't seem to take a very big change of state to get the paranormal activity in my house going! Last night I framed and hung up an Army Air Corps group photo from 1945 that belonged to my great uncle. I've had the photo in my house for several weeks. Well, the simple act of hanging it on the wall was enough to cause strange things to happen. Two times this evening I looked and the photo was moved across the hall, off of the wall, on the floor propped up against the wall. I know no one else is moving it, because it's happened while I've been alone in the apartment!! If it had fallen of the wall it wouldn't have made it clear across the hall, let alone land propped up against the wall! Also, the planchette on the 1920's ouija board has been moving on it's own again. I was watching We Were Soldiers this evening. After the I finished watching the movie I called my mom and told her what I thought about it (I thought it was an excellent movie!), and about the time I was hanging up the phone I heard a scraping sound on the table. I looked over and the planchette was moving. And not just shaking, actually moving! This was the first time I've actually seen something being moved like this. It didn't spell anything out, it just slowly moved across the board and fell on the floor. I picked it up and it felt cold, like ice. I have to admit this really freaked me out. The ceiling fan wasn't on, so I can't blame that (not that it would be strong enough to move the big wooden planchette anyway). The board was laying flat, so I can't rationalize it by blaming it on that either. Strange stuff, wonder how well I'll sleep tonight?!?!?!

Speaking of sleep, I really haven't been sleeping well. I've been having really strange dreams. A few nights ago I dreamed that I was going blind in my left eye. The doctor told me I was going to be blind in that eye for the rest of my life because the tick sucked all of the blood out of it and there was no way to get blood circulating in my eye ever again. Also, he said there was no way to remove the tick, I'd just have to wait until it fell off. Talk about a nightmare!

What a crazy day! The craziness started right away this morning, even before I went to work. I woke up about 25 minutes before my normal waking time because I thought it sounded like something fell in my room. I looked around briefly in my half awake/half asleep state and didn't notice anything out of place so I cursed under my breath about waking up before I needed to and laid back down. At first I thought nothing of it, but then I suddenly I thought "Oh my god, I have a mouse under my dresser"!!!! A few seconds passed before I started to panic too much (I'm not scared of mice but I'm not really prepared to catch any) when I suddenly had another thought....it probably wasn't a mouse, it was probably an African Clawed Frog (the totally aquatic ones). I got up and looked in the aquarium. Sure enough, Joan was nowhere to be seen! I took both of the bottom dresser drawers out and checked there first, but there was nothing under there. For the next 15-20 minutes I tore up the rest of my bedroom looking for the lost frog. I couldn't find her anywhere! I kept remembering the rustling/crinkling sound I heard under the dresser, so I reluctantly decided to pull the heavy dresser out from the wall.
