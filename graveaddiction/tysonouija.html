My Ghostly Admirer

July 23, 2007



On July 23, 2007 I received an e-mail from Tyson about an interesting ouija board experience:


Ever since we moved into our house in Southeastern Ohio when I was six years-old, I've been seeing a woman in a white gown standing by my bedside. This happens once every six months, however, I haven't seen her for two years. Wondering if she still was in my house, I borrowed my friend's Ouija Board.

I call this "my ghostly admirer."

Me: May I please speak with the spirit who haunts this house?
Board: Yes.

Me: Is this the spirit?
Board: 2.

Me: There are two spirits in my house?
Board: Yes.

Me: May I speak with the woman I've seen?
Board: Here.

Me: Is this you?
Board: Yes.

Me: Will you spell your name out for me?
Board: June.

Me: Is your name June?
Board: Yes.

Me: What is the other spirit's name?
Board: Don't know.

Me: Is it a man or woman?
Board: Don't know.

Me: Why am I the only person in my family who can see you?
Board: Son.

Me: Do you think I am your son?
Board: No.

Me: Do I remind you of your son?
Board: Yes.

Me: Do you know my name?
Board: No.

Me: Do you want to know my name?
Board: Yes.

Me: My name is Tyson. It's nice to meet you, June.
Board: You 2.

Me: June, are you scared of crossing over to the spirit world?
Board: No.

Me: Then why do you stay here?
Board: My house.

Me: But my family lives here now.
Board: Together.

Me: You want you and my family to live together?
Board: (circled around "yes" two times)

Me: June, do you know that you scare me?
Board: Sorry.

Me: June, why do you watch me sleep?
Board: Peacef.

Me: I'm peaceful?
Board: Yes.

Me: June, will you please cross over to the other side, for me?
Board: no movement

Me: June, are you there?
Board: Goodbye.

After that intimate session with June, I was a bit creeped out. I was alone, which I know I should not have done. That night while laying in my bed, listening to my radio and slowly falling asleep, I heard someone say my name. I sat up and looked around. I turned off the radio, and I heard it again. It was more like a sudden "...aaaaAAAaaa...," but I could still hear my name in it. I have a feeling I haven't seen the last of June.
