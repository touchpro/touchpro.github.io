I haven't posted for a few days since nothing too exciting has happened. I've been busy at work, but the last two days went really slow. That's unusual since the really busy days usually go by fast!

Tuesday night I received an e-mail to my Grave Addiction e-mail address that contained some very exciting news. About a month ago I applied to join the Ohio Exploration Society. It's a group for people who like to explore Ohio's abandoned/forgotten/haunted/historical places (which includes cemeteries!). The e-mail I received on Tuesday was the e-mail with my acceptance letter!! So now I'm a member of the OES!! Grave Addiction really helped me get accepted since the site proves I do have some exploration experience. There aren't any explorations planned right now due to the weather, but once it warms up/stops snowing I'll get to go on my first OES exploration. This is so exciting!

Another exciting thing...on Wednesday I purchased an antique wooden Ouija board that was made in the 1920's. It even has a wooden planchette (message indicator). Antique Ouija boards are one of the most difficult board games to find. It makes sense though. Think about it...if someone had a Candyland game that they didn't want anymore they'd throw it away or give it to someone else. Now a lot of people getted freaked out after using a ouija board, so instead of giving it away or simply throwing it in the trash, they'd chop it into pieces or burn it so the "evil" spirit couldn't contact them again. Anyway, I can't wait to try this new (well, new to me anyway!) board out! I think John, Ryan, and Amanda are going to join me for a "contact the dead party" (that's what Ryan called it!) sometime next week. I am really curious to see if we end up talking to my great grandma (or a spirit claiming to be her) again. Of course, I don't know who owned this board before me so I guess there could be other spirits tied to it. The people reading this who don't believe in spirits/ghosts probably think we're crazy!

It's still snowing....it doesn't seem like it will ever end! I went out to scrape/brush the 6 inches of snow off the truck a few hours ago, but you can't tell I did anything by looking at it now. My sister and I loved it when we got lots of snow. We could throw snowballs at each other, sled down the hill, feed Tillie and Cherokee (the outside dogs) snow, and build snowmen. Now that I have to go to work and drive in it, I don't get as excited about snow. But since I didn't have to drive in it this weekend I kind of enjoyed it.

This afternoon I watched a special called "Biography of a Corpse" on the National Geographic Channel. It takes a lot to make me disgusted (besides needles and eye injuries of course...I can never watch that stuff), but this show succeeded in disgusting me. It was about this place (I think it's in Tennessee) that studies the decomposition of corpses. They take bodies donated to them (or bodies that remain unclaimed) and put them outside to study how long it takes them to decompose. They focused on one body from start to finish, and the part that made me sick was the part where they showed the maggots and insects eating away the body. They showed the maggots in the guy's mouth, nose, eyes, and ears. It was really quite disgusting!! However, at the same time I found it fascinating so I didn't change the channel. I know it's a good/necessary thing, but I can't imagine wanting to donate my body for something like that.

Talking about that show made me think of another show I watched recently. The other night I watched "Cannibal: The Real Hannibal Lecters" on HBO. That show was interesting too. The most disturbing person the host profiled was this guy in Japan who is still living. He killed and ate a girl in France years ago, and they sent him back to Japan without saying that he should remain in a psychiatric hospital. Now he's a free man, and he's actually treated like a celebrity. He does restaurant reviews and stars in porn movies. He told the girl interviewing him that he won't kill again, but he still dreams about eating people. Kind of scary!

I also want to mention that I found a really cool Ouija board site while surfing today: The Museum of Talking Boards. There is a lot of great info, including "Directions on How to Use the Ouija Board" and "Ouijastitions"!

For example, hold your left hand in front of you and hold all of your fingers and thumb close together. Move your thumb to the right so it's sticking out...that's what my foot looks like when it has a foot cramp.
