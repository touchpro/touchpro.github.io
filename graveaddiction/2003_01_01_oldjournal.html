Speaking of cemeteries, I mentioned in my last post that one of the cemeteries I visited was in Haydenville, OH. The cemetery I visited there was Wolfe Cemetery. It is at the top of a hill, and it's famous (at least in the world of people with cemetery fetishes!) for a grave that's surrounded by an iron fence. The grave is rather large, there's a small headstone at the top of a long concrete slab. The whole thing is surrounded by a rusty iron fence, one of the fences that have the spikey things at the top. Rumor says that a witch was burned there, and that her body is buried in that grave. If you touch the grave you're supposed to feel heat, and if you lay on it you'll be sucked into Hell! Now I thought it would look pretty silly to lay on a grave (plus it'd be kind of disrespectful), but I did touch it. It was covered in snow, and I didn't feel any heat which was a little disappointing. However, a few minutes later my left hand (the one I touched the grave with) felt EXTREMELY hot. My right hand felt normal. I thought maybe the hot feeling was just a result of my hand finally warming up from being in the snow, but it lasted for the rest of the weekend. I made everyone feel it, and everyone agreed that it did seem hot. I'm still trying to find a logical explanation, but I'm afraid there might not be one!!

Oh, I almost forgot...I have another wierd "haunted tablecloth" story. Last weekend I was burning some candles, like I always do on the weekend. I have three tealight holders that sit on the bookshelf, on top of the tablecloth. The middle tealight stopped burning about two hours before the other two, because the wick burnt weird and got buried under the wax...none of the wick was sticking out. Then about four hours later later the other two burnt down and went out. An hour or so later I was sitting on the couch when I saw smoke coming out from what I thought was the middle tealight. But that was impossible since there was no wick left to burn, so I got up to check it out. I walked over to the bookshelf and there was no doubt that the middle tealight candle was indeed smoking, but there was no flame or wick. There was quite a bit of smoke. I checked the other two tealights to make sure it wasn't coming from them, but like I said, they went out oer an hour before this happened, all that was left were the little metal things that hold the wicks. Then it just stopped. I was wide awake, so I wasn't imagining it. Pretty weird, isn't it?!?
