Tuesday, April 08, 2003
posted at 9:43:57 PM

Another fun and exciting week at work. So far this week is a little better than last, but not that much better! Maybe it's the colder weather that's causing me to feel a bit down.

Anyway, this past weekend was the Civil War Encampment at the park near my parents' house. It was quite interesting. But it seems a little funny to me when I think that these people get so serious about pretending to be Civil War soldiers...they camped out in tents during a thunderstorm!! Of course, some probably think that my cemetery addiction is strange too. Oh, and don't forget my frog obsession...I know people think that's stange! But back to the Civil War...they had muskets and three cannons that they fired for the artillery demos, which really made my parents' dogs bark LOTS! I took some pictures, I'll be posting them on Grave Addiction within the next few days. Also, Jason and Misty from the Ohio Exploration Society came down and they took some pictures as well.

Speaking of Grave Addiction, I made some major updates to the site last night. You can see the list of updates here.

Mark and I will be going to Point Pleasant, WV for a short trip this weekend to take some more pictures of the TNT Area for the Mothman section of Grave Addiction. We're also going to visit the Lakin Psychiatric Hospital that Jeremy told me about and the Tyn Rhos Cemetery that Mark said is really creepy. I can't wait! So far it looks like the weather is going to be decent so we should have a good time exploring.

No more significant hauntings in my apartment to talk about. The other night all of my remote controls (5 total) were arranged in a circle in the middle of my living room. And as usual, the "normal" stuff...the candleholders moving, the Ouija board moving, etc. I've been seeing a lot of shadows recently though, and the cold spots around the apartment (that move) have been more noticeable.

In a few weeks I'm volunteering with a cemetery clean-up project. There's an old cemetery in Upper Arlington, Bickett-Richards, that's in really bad shape. The county has decided to clean it up. I'm going to help with gardening tasks, like planting bulbs/plants and mulching. I think it'll be fun...I love volunteering and I love cemeteries so this is the perfect combination! I stopped by the cemetery tonight (which is kind of hard to find if you don't know exactly where to look) and took some "before" pictures. I'll be adding them to Grave Addiction soon. I also want to take some "after" pictures for the site too.

Tuesday, April 01, 2003
posted at 9:17:14 PM

Is this week ever going to end????

Obviously, it hasn't been a great week. I'm OK when I'm at home, but at work I've been a little down lately. But doesn't everyone have weeks like that? I've noticed my coworkers having "bad" weeks before, but for some reason I guess it's not OK for me to have a bad week. Lots of people have been asking "are you OK, you haven't been as talkative as usual", "are you in a bad mood, you don't seem as perky as usual", etc. And on Monday my manager actually asked me to stay after a meeting, just to ask if I was doing OK, someone told him that I seemed depressed last Friday (he was out so I guess someone felt it was necessary to inform him)! I wonder if everyone gets these questions when they're having a bad week? It's starting to annoy me, which most definitely isn't helping me feel more "perky" or talkative. I'm just a bit overloaded with project assignments right now, maybe once some of them are wrapped up I'll feel better.

The other thing at work that absolutely drove me CRAZY this week was the never ending report. On Sunday a few of us were in to work on some Statement issues. Deidee needed to print out a report, but she accidentally printed the wrong one. The report she accidentally printed was about 150,000 lines!!! Since we printed it from the SAR application it didn't show up in the task bar on her computer, usually we can stop these jobs from printing by hitting the "cancel print" button on the printer. It appeared that this solution worked until about an hour later when the report mysteriously started printing again, from the beginning even! We called tons of people, even our Data Center, and no one could help us! It was kinda funny on Sunday. However, it started to print again on Monday. That's when everyone started complaining about how much paper was being wasted, etc. And we still couldn't find anyone to help us with getting the report from hell to stop printing! Then this morning I heard someone complaining at the printer and I got a sick feeling in my stomach...yes, it was the dreaded report. It started to print a few more times today, but by 11:00 I got ahold of someone who was able to stop it from printing!!! So at least now that nightmare is over!

Since I think a few of my coworkers do read this occasionally I better stop complaining about work related stuff :) Let's move on, shall we...

OK, first let me tell you that I'll understand if you call me a dumbass and tell me that I deserve everything that's happened. Now that I've said that, I'll tell you about my most recent ghost related experiences. Two years ago I saw two pieces of a broken tombstone in my great uncle's yard...now part of a park. The piece with the name is missing, the only info I know is that the person died in 1867, and I can make part of an age. As far as we know there was not a cemetery on the property. Turns out that the park wasn't going to keep the tombstone since they had no idea where it came from, they were just going to throw it away. So I decided to bring it home this weekend, it'll look nice in my garden. I got home late on Saturday, and I was feeling too tired and lazy to put the tombstone pieces outside in the garden so I just sat it on my table in the living room. Other than feeling intense cold spots throughout the apartment (even though I turned the furnace up to 68 degrees!), nothing strange happened. Sunday I spent most of my day at work. I got home around 5 PM, so it was still light outside. When I walked into my apartment the first thing that I noticed was that the living room seemed really bright! I knew I left the patio blinds shut that morning, so I wasn't sure why the room was so bright. Then I saw that the blinds were all formed into "X" patterns! I do have a heating vent over there, but all that has ever done was make the bottoms of the blinds shake a little. Also, the vent wouldn't cause the blinds to be moved into the "X" patterns! In addition to the blinds, a few things had been moved around, like candleholders, the Ouija board planchette...you know, the "normal" stuff!

I know what you're thinking - did I put the tombstone outside after the blind incident? No, I ate some dinner and went to bed because work wore me out. I woke up Monday morning and everything was fine, nothing had been moved. However, when I came home from work Monday afternoon everything wasn't normal! I have a bad habit of kicking off my shoes by the front door and leaving them there, so unless I'm expecting company there's always a pile of shoes near my front door. Well, Monday afternoon the five pairs of shoes that were laying by the door had been moved. All ten shoes were lined up single file from the edge of my living room towards the patio door. I saw it as a sign - the tombstone wanted to be outside! I didn't waste anytime, I found a place for it in my garden and it was outside within half an hour after I arrived home!

Tonight nothing too strange happened. Just the "normal" stuff!

By the way, I think I forgot to post this last week...I put the list of questions and answers from the last Ouija board party out on Grave Addiction. We used the board for about an hour and thirty-three minutes and had a lot of interesting stuff come out...CLICK HERE to read about it!
