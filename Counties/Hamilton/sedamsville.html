Sedamsville Rectory

Priests affiliated with Our Lady of Perpetual Help lived in the adjacent Sedamsville Rectory beginning in the nineteenth century. Sometime before 1900 a priest, Father Donald MacLeod, was struck and killed by a train while crossing the tracks on foot nearby; his ghost remained in the rectory. Interestingly, the basement supposedly saw use later on as a dogfighting ring.
