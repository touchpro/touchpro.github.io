PICTURES

REC ROOM

This is the famous zebra or tiger striped carpet that everyone writes about. I had heard about this before I had heard about the house being potentially abandoned. The news covered this carpet. This may be the most famous carpet in Trumbell County. This is a fairly large Rec Room. There was still some furniture placed in the center, but I'm not sure if that was original. There is a wet bar, stairs to the master bedroom, and doors leading to the main house as well as the pool area.

 
The stairs to the right lead to the Master Bedroom.
This is a projection TV that is way larger than it looks. I would guess its around 6ft tall.
A wet bar in the middle of the room.
Mondern furniture
The spiral stairs lead to the basement. The doors lead to the main house directly to the living room. If I had to guess I'd say the rec room was an addition to the original house.
To the basement.
Check the lights!
This is the entry way to the pool and rec room, the door was wide open with snow blowing in.
Fridge and whatnot in the bar.
Even the gumball machine was gold... not real gold obviously, but gold none the less.

 

Living Room/Dining Room

This is the first room of the main house that I saw. The stairs lead to the upper level where the Master Bedroom was located. You can look down in to the family room area from the top of the steps. The carpet was super stained, and obviously this is a little outdated, but this room was actually kinda nice in real life.

 
Directly ahead is the main entry to the house. Upstairs is the Master Bedroom. Through the arch is the turret.
The door at the top is the master bedroom. That is a dining/living area through the arch... maybe a parlor?
Fireplace. The white round things appear to have been picture or light holders.
