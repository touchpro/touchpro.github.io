The Long Tunnel

This is the portal for the 2nd tunnel. This one was much easier to get in to than the short tunnel. We simply climbed up the gate and back down the other side. There were some construction workers near by that we had to watch out for, but we were otherwise out of view.


This sign is right inside the first tunnel. It is a warning of the high pressure in the water pipe housed inside the tunnel on the other side. The water pipe was put in the tunnel long after the subway project was abandoned. It's said that these tunnels saved the city hundreds of thousands in construction on the pipeline.


I'm not sure what this lift looking device was for. It's possible that it was for laying the pipes in to place, but I doubt it. This was in the tunnel that housed the pipe. Most of our journey was taken in the other tunnel that runs next to it. Windows connect them throughout nearly the entire trip (One station is the only exception).


This view was taken shortly after entering the tunnel. It's a view of the gate that we came through. Most of the pictures here are dark like this one. Hopefully this picture will help give a scope as to the size of the tunnels.


Here is where the pipe comes up in to the tunnel. The pipe was laid in the late 1950s. The pipe is covered by thick plastic to keep the dripping water from eating away at the pipe's surface I'm assuming.


This is another picture of the pipe farther down. You can see light from one of the vents farther down. There were vents throughout much of the system, however some were constructed as to not let light in easily. There are other sections with no vents, and no light at all.


This is a look down the tunnel that I think would run South (without the pipe). You can see the wooden elevations in the floor that the track would have laid on. These were removed in some spots. In other spots you could see how the track would be banked for the turns. HERE is one example.


This is one of many old fuse panels and switch boxes throughout the tunnels. Most of them were broken like this one. HERE is another. There were also new switch boxes for the fiberoptic lines running through the tunnel. The tubes for these lines can be seen in the above picture.
