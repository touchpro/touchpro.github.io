The Short Tunnel

Although much of the content of this Site is focused on Columbus, the actual intent is to cover all of Ohio, or at least as much as possible. Given that fact, it was impossible to resist the Cincinnati abandoned Subway tunnels. Of course, as with all of our explorations, we did screw this up a little.


Many of these pictures are really bad because of the lack of light in the tunnels. This tunnel had a little more light than the one on the following pages because it was much shorter. This tunnel was cut off during the making of I-75. Unfortunately we didn't realize this until we were already in there.


Climbing in though the gate that has been patched many times proved to be the most difficult entrance to date. We got scraped, snagged, and injured in ways I didn't know possible. Once we were in we were disappointed to find out that we were in a tunnel that ended some 100 yards later.


We turned around as advised by the message above. This picture is taken looking toward our exit. Exiting through the hole we came in through proved to be more difficult than entering. I think I remember being upside down and being able to see the bottom of my feet at one point. FUN!!!
