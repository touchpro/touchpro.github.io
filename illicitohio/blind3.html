The Colombus School for the Blind

Stairways and Elevators

There were cool ass stair cases everywhere in the Blind School. The upper floors had been blocked off during the time that the Highway Patroll occupied the building. There was even a note that said you could only access the top floors with permission. We couldn't find anyone to ask.


The stairs to the left are from the highway patrol days, all of the other stairs pictured come from the days of the Blind School. Its interesting to think of why they would put steep narrow stair cases in a school for blind people. Its even more interesting to think what it must have looked like at lunch time.


The stairs to the right were very very narrow. This one of my favorite stair cases to date. The ere small, narrow, and had a bend, what more could you want? They led to this strange hot and smelly room where "charles" and I are guessing the old towers were attached before they were taken down. I'm guessing the stairs took you to the room, then on to the towers.


Here is that room. It was very hot, and smelled like warm wood. I'm guessing it burst in to flames about 2 seconds after we left, because it felt like it was going to then. One had this fireplace with some writing on the wall behind it. The other was a bare room (there were 2 narrow stairs, each with a small room at the top). The roof was peaked because at this point you are in the attic. .


This is the coveted Elevator Room. I feel that a building hasn't been explored unless you run across the elevator room at some point. If the building doesn't have elvators, then you can skip looking for the elevator room. I think the most recent date on the inspection was 1992. This is around the time the the OSHP quit using it.



I put these pictures together because they are taken upright. I don't know why I do that because it always screws up the website layout. On the left is one of the doors added by the Highway Patroll I'm guessing. It requires keycard access. On the right is the elevator doors as seen on the 3rd floor.
