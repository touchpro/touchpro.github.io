The First Station

I was most excited about seeing one of the stations. The first station we found was a little bit of a let down. It still was unbelievable to think that we were under Cincinnati in an unused subway station. How different it would look if there were people in here waiting for a train. Of course we'd be dead if that were the case!


The two openings lead to the stairways. I'm not sure of the exact location of this tunnel because I know nothing about Cincinnati or the location of anything there. If I remember right it was about 15 minutes in to the tunnel that we found this station, so if anyone knows the location feel free to let me know.


Here are the stairs. I was hoping we'd be down far enough that there would be stairs, a landing with a turn, then more stairs like in NY and DC subways. Unfortunately the subway isn't that low.


Here's another view of the stairs that have been capped off by cement. It appears that the cement top is at ground level. I'm curious if there are any signs of this on the top side or if there is a road of building there now.


This is looking across the station, rails, center wall, and on to the station on the other side. I didn't venture over there but I'm guessing it looked about the same as the station we were in. It was difficult to get over there so we left it alone.
