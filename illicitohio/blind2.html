The Colombus School for the Blind

The Columbus School for the Blind The Columbus Ohio School for the blind was one of our best visits to date. I know that I say that every time, but as a building, the blind school is one of the oldest, and greatest we've seen. We'd been wanting to check it out for some time, and the chance finally came up late one saturday night.


We had planned a group trip to somewhere in Columbus. Originally it was to be a large cast of "explorers" from Columbus. Unfortunately only about five people made it, so we went on a small group voyage. I missed the others, but a well placed note let me know where to find them. THANKS GUYS!


Now I know this will suprise those who have read about past trips, and my wonderful luck,but my camera broke that saturday night, so "charlie" and I returned to take the pictures you see here. Because of the darkness the previous night, it was a bit difficult to take good pictures anyhow.


The Roof

It didn't take long for us to find the roof. We had to clib some questionable steps up to a small crawl space that eventually led out on to the roof by way of an escape hatch. Since it had been storming, the roof probably wasn't the safest place to be. The view however was great.



Personally I can't make out crap in the pictures except a bunch of buildings and houses. The one that should have the seneca is the one below. It is also the worst in quality, so good luck finding anything. The roof was still one of the many highlights of the trip. It can't be captured in a picture.
